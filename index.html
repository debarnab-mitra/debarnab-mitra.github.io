About me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the Electrical and Computer Engineering Department at UCLA. I am fortunate to work in the Laboratory for Robust Information Systems (LORIS) under the supervision of Prof. Lara Dolecek. My research interests include information theory, channel coding, and its applications to blockchains and non-volatile memories. I am currently working on designing application specific LDPC codes for blockchain systems.

In past, I have worked with Prof. Sibi Raj B Pillai and Prof. Himanshu Asnani on understanding the sum-capacity of Compound Multiple Access Channels.

I earned my M.S. degree from the ECE Department at UCLA in 2020, for which I was awarded the Distinguished MS Thesis Award in Signals and Systems. I graduated from IIT Bombay with a B. Tech. (Hons.) in Electrical Engineering and a minor in Computer Science and Engineering in 2018.
